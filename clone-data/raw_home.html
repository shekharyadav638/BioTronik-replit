    The new Sky family of devices introduces solutions that address a broad range of clinical challenges, including Left Bundle Branch Area Pacing (LBBAP). It features a new anti-tachycardia pacing (ATP) suite to expand atrial and ventricular ATP options, and continues to offer BIOTRONIK’s unique DX Technology, the only single-lead solution providing true atrial sensing without an additional atrial lead.

  • 100,000 DX Devices Implanted Worldwide

    BIOTRONIK’s DX Technology has reached a remarkable milestone – 100,000 DX devices implanted worldwide.14 Backed by a rich history of clinical studies since 2009, that led to extensive evidence, this accomplishment underscores our unwavering dedication to innovation and patient care.

    Available as a unique, single-lead ICD system equipped with a floating atrial dipole, DX ICDs:

    • offer reliable, timely information about atrial arrhythmias and detect AF early.2, 3, 4, 5, 6
    • help clinicians monitor AF burden over time.3

    In combination with BIOTRONIK Home Monitoring® and optionally the BIOTRONIK Patient App, this AF management solution offers a comprehensive assessment of ICD patients and enables earlier, well-informed decision-making.3

  • Prospera™ Spinal Cord Stimulation System with Embrace One™

    BIOTRONIK Neuro’s Prospera spinal cord stimulation (SCS) system treats patients suffering from chronic pain, helping them embrace life again. The system uniquely features:

    • RESONANCE™, the first and only multiphase stimulation paradigm
    • Embrace One, a truly first-of-its-kind ecosystem that provides automatic, objective, remote daily monitoring

    Prospera SCS System is approved in the US only and is not available outside of the US.

    Note: Embrace One is a support platform intended to help manage a patient’s experience with spinal cord stimulation. It is not intended to be used for medical diagnosis or medical treatment.

  • Giving Control Back To The Patient: CLS and the Return to Physiologic Control Webinar

    Conduction System Pacing has kept the goal of 'physiologic pacing' at the forefront of cardiac rhythm in recent years. But what about physiologic heart rate?


    We invite you to come learn from Drs. Matthew Evans, Jonathan Hsu, and David Hayes as they explore how BIOTRONIK's proprietary algorithm (CLS) gives heart rate control back to the patient's autonomic nervous system. 

    What You'll Learn:

    • How CLS puts the patient back in control of rate
    • Recent clinical evidence showing CLS' role in reducing subclinical AF, improving quality of life and restoring chronotropic competence.
    • A special focus on CLS impact on Heart Failure patients

    BIOTRONIK Announces First U.S. Implant of Investigational Next-Generation Leadless Pacemaker

    BIOTRONIK, a global leader in cardiovascular medical technology, today announced the first U.S. implant of its LivIQ leadless pacemaker as part of the BIO-LivIQ global pivotal clinical trial. This next-generation single-device solution is designed to deliver atrioventricular (AV) synchrony through advanced electrical atrial far-field sensing. The implant was successfully performed by James Ip, MD, FACC, FHRS, Professor of Medicine, Director of Cardiac Pacing and Implantable Devices and Principal Investigator at Weill Cornell Medicine and a Cardiac Electrophysiologist at NewYork-Presbyterian
